The Division

Flight as both image and instrument.

Air is the aerial operations division of Elevate Frame Group. It exists to serve two realities at once: the need for premium aerial visuals and the need for reliable operational visibility. We approach flight not as novelty, but as a disciplined capability that can support storytelling, site intelligence, inspection pathways, technical documentation, and enterprise expansion.

Our current stack supports controlled, professional flight across training, production, and mission-ready deployment. The roadmap includes RTK precision, thermal pathways, and enterprise systems designed to serve infrastructure, land, agricultural, and industrial use cases with more depth over time.

The Fleet

Built beyond toy-drone thinking.

Air is structured around professional development, layered flight capability, and a real-world progression from training platforms to enterprise-grade systems. The objective is to deploy the right aircraft, the right workflow, and the right discipline for the mission.

Cinema Platform

DJI Inspire 3

Professional cinema drone with interchangeable lens system. The premium aerial cinema platform for high-end visual production. Features raw capture, six-directional obstacle sensing, and dual-operator mode.

Production Platform

DJI Mavic 3 Classic

Hasselblad camera, 4/3 CMOS sensor. Versatile production platform for cinematic aerial work, site visibility, and commercial engagements. A proven field-ready workhorse.

Training & Agility Platform

DJI Mini 4 Pro (×2)

Sub-250g classification, obstacle avoidance, RC 2 controller. Primary pilot training and agility development platform — how pilots earn reps on modern, professional systems without compromising quality.

FPV & Dynamic Platform

DJI Avata 2

FPV with goggles and motion controller. Immersive FPV capability for dynamic, tight-space, and cinematic movement work — a distinct visual language no other platform provides.

Compliance & Standards

Professional flight requires discipline.

Air is built around lawful operation, mission awareness, equipment readiness, and a disciplined respect for risk. The goal is not just to fly — the goal is to fly with purpose, consistency, and standards that support long-term trust across commercial, institutional, and technical environments.

All commercial drone operations are conducted under FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot Certificate requirements. Operations are logged, insured, and mission-planned with full compliance documentation available on request.
